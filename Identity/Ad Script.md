## Structure

Four beats. Clean pace. Room for visuals to breathe.

|Beat|Duration|Purpose|
|---|---|---|
|1 — The Feeling|7–8 seconds|Make the viewer feel seen before saying a word|
|2 — The Reframe|10–12 seconds|The insight that separates Spindle from everything else|
|3 — The Tool|10–12 seconds|What Spindle is and what it gives you|
|4 — The Close|5 seconds|The line they will remember|

Total: 32–37 seconds. Leaves room for breathing at the edit.

---

## Script

---

### Beat 1 — The Feeling

_Voiceover, slow. No music yet — or music barely present, low._

> You had the plan. You meant it. You started.
> 
> And somehow, three years passed.

_Visual: a blank journal. An unopened app. A goal written and crossed out. Simple, recognizable, no narration needed — the visuals carry the weight. One image per line._

---

### Beat 2 — The Reframe

_Voiceover, same calm pace. Music begins to build very slightly._

> The problem was never the plan. It was never the motivation.
> 
> Real change is not a burst. It is a garment — woven day by day, thread by thread.

_Visual: a single thread. Then two. Then a cloth beginning to take shape — slowly, visibly, honestly. The weaving is the metaphor made literal. This is the center of the ad. Give it time._

---

### Beat 3 — The Tool

_Voiceover, slightly warmer. Music present now but still restrained._

> To weave, you need a spindle.
> 
> Not a motivator. Not a streak counter. A spindle — the tool that holds the thread so the weaving can begin.
> 
> You start as an apprentice. One day, you become Penelope.

_Visual: the Spindle app interface — brief, clean, unforced. The garment visual on a commitment detail screen. Then the progression — not all levels named, just the sense of a journey from beginning to mastery. End on the name: Spindle._

---

### Beat 4 — The Close

_Voiceover, quiet and certain. Music settles._

> Weave your life, one thread at a time.

_Visual: app icon. Clean hold. Fade._

## Direction Notes

**Tone:** calm, serious, craftsman-like. Never excited. Never punishing. The voice of someone who has learned that real change is quiet daily work. Think documentary narrator, not advertising voice.

**Pace:** slow enough that each line lands before the next begins. The instinct will be to speed up — resist it. The unhurried pace is what makes this different from every other app ad.

**Music:** minimal. Something textural and warm — not motivational, not sad. The kind of music that feels like early morning work. It should support the voice, not compete with it.

**Visuals:** the weaving animation is the most important visual asset in this ad. Actual threads appearing one by one, a cloth taking shape slowly — this image should be held long enough to feel real. Do not cut away too quickly. The garment becoming visible is the entire message made visual.

**The Penelope line:** "One day, you become Penelope" should land without explanation. Do not define her. Viewers who know will feel it. Viewers who do not will want to know. Both responses are correct.

---

## What This Ad Does Not Do

- Does not show a transformation montage
- Does not use before/after imagery
- Does not promise results
- Does not name competitors
- Does not list features
- Does not use exclamation marks

The ad sells an understanding of how change works — not the app itself. If the viewer leaves with the feeling that they have been told something true that they had not quite named before, the ad has done its job.

## Variations

**15-second cut — social media:** Beat 2 only, ending on the close line. The reframe is the strongest single moment — it can stand alone.

> Real change is not a burst. It is a garment — woven day by day, thread by thread. _Spindle._ Weave your life, one thread at a time.

**Story format (vertical, 9:16):** Same four beats, visuals adapted for vertical. Each beat gets its own card. Text on screen replaces or supplements voiceover for silent viewing.
